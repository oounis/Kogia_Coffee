# Pricing Model

> **Assumption flag:** Owner figures — premium beans **37 DT/kg**, medium **32 DT/kg** — are treated here as **bean cost (COGS input)**. If 37/32 are meant as *retail* prices instead, tell me and I'll re-base the whole table.

## 1. Cost basis (per 1 kg of finished blend)
Blends mix premium coffee with cheaper roasted grains (barley, chickpea ≈ 4–6 DT/kg) and spices (cardamom/saffron are the pricey ones). Indicative finished-blend cost:

| Blend | Coffee % | Est. blend COGS / kg | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Djerbi Signature | ~57% | ~24 DT | grains lower cost, modest spice |
| Ethiopian Classic | ~93% | ~35 DT | mostly premium Arabica |
| Khaleeji Gold | ~100% | ~40 DT | saffron + heavy cardamom |
| Marrakech Spice | ~85% | ~31 DT | spice-heavy |
| Sweet Dunes | ~75% | ~27 DT | barley + date lower cost |
| Sahara Ginger | ~85% | ~30 DT | ginger-heavy |

*(COGS excludes packaging, labour, roasting gas, overhead — add those before final margin.)*

**Logic:** small bags carry a premium per-gram (convenience + trial). 500 g ≈ 57% of 1 kg price; 250 g ≈ 33%.

## 4. Break-even sketch
- If fixed monthly cost (rent + utilities + 1 salary) ≈ **2,000 DT** and avg margin per 1 kg ≈ **22 DT**, break-even ≈ **~91 kg/month** (~3 kg/day) *plus* machine cup income.
