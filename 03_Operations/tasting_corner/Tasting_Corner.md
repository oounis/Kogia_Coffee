# Tasting Corner

Where browsing becomes buying. A small, beautiful bar where staff guide a customer to *their* blend.

## Setup
- 2–3 stools at a **zellige-tiled mini-bar**.
- A **dallah** (Arabic pot) + clay/glass cups for the traditional pour.
- The 6 blends in labelled jars + a printed **taste map**.
- A spittoon/rinse + water (palate reset).

## The script (staff)
1. "How do you take your coffee — light, sweet, spicy, or strong?"
2. Pour the matching blend (small cup).
3. Talk through what's in it (cardamom, ginger, barley…). Transparency builds trust.
4. Offer the **tasting flight (3 blends, 5 DT)** — *credited toward any bag they buy.*
5. Close: hand them the bag of their favourite.

## Why it works
- Free/cheap taste removes the risk of buying powder blind.
- Guided choice → confidence → bigger basket (often 2 bags + gift box).
- It's the most photogenic spot → social reach.

## KPIs to log
- Tastings/day, taste-to-bag conversion %, avg bags per converted taster.
