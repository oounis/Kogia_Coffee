# Shop Ambiance & Atmosphere

Goal: a small space that feels **warm, authentic, and premium** — and is impossible not to photograph. The atmosphere does the selling; the powder is the souvenir.

## The feeling we want
"A modern Djerbi spice-house." Whitewashed Mediterranean meets warm spice-market: clean and bright, but rich with the smell of roasting coffee and the colour of spices in jars.

## Materials & textures
- **Whitewashed walls** + one **raw-wood or terracotta feature wall**.
- **Brass / dark-wood shelving** lined with **glass jars** of whole spices and beans (visual + smell).
- **Jute, linen, and clay** accents (the Djerba pottery identity).
- A **live roasting/grinding corner** visible to customers — sight + sound + smell = trust.

## Lighting
- **Warm, low** ambient light (2700K), layered: small pendant lamps over the counter, hidden LED strip warming the shelves.
- One bright **"hero" spotlight** on the tasting table for photos.

## Zones (small footprint)
1. **Window / hero shelf** — the 6 blends in beautiful bags, front-lit. First thing you see.
2. **Coin machine** — by the entrance, impulse trial. Signage: *"Insert coin → taste a tradition."*
3. **Tasting corner** — 2–3 stools, a small zellige-tiled bar, the dallah & cups. Where conversion happens.
4. **Roast/grind corner** — the theatre. Bags filled and labelled in front of the customer.
5. **Counter / pickup** — pay, wrap, hand over.

## Sensory checklist (the 5 senses)
- **Smell:** roasting coffee + cardamom in the air (this is the #1 sales tool).
- **Sound:** soft Tunisian/oud playlist + the grinder.
- **Sight:** spice jars, warm light, beautiful bags, one bold wall.
- **Touch:** jute bags, clay cups, wooden scoops.
- **Taste:** the free/cheap tasting flight.

## Social-media built-in
- A **branded photo wall** (logo + "Kogia Coffee · Djerba") for selfies.
- Every bag and cup is designed to look good in a photo.
- A small chalk menu board with the day's machine blends.
